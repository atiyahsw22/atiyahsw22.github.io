i guess i'll always be a dreamer: a short story of loneliness

i heard him, i didn't just want to dance.



[dreamer, atiyah saleemah, 2026]

it goes without question that i love michael jackson and anything jackson related. i’ve been a fan of his and the family since i was seven years old. it is actually michael jackson who sparked my maladaptive daydreaming and my vast imagination. i can remember being a little girl in the backseat of my mom’s car, staring out the window into the night's sky as i listened to “maybe tomorrow” and i imagined myself dating little michael; he was my first real celebrity crush outside of arnez from one on one.

most kids like me [at that time] have gone through the inevitable m.j. phase...they might not have gone as far as to dating him in their head but you get my jest. generations of children continue to go through this phase, eons after michael’s prime years and well into his unfortunate death. the new biopic starring his nephew jaafar has since elongated his legacy to precede any of us with the constant saying that “michael jackson has fans not even born yet,” deeming to be true each day.

the personal lens of the film created scenes that felt far more soul reaching than the score had. we as fans connected less with michael the machine and more with michael the man. i felt so deeply touched by the movie that i sat in the theatre long after the ending credits, simply blown away by not what i just saw but what i instantly felt; relief.

there’s a scene... you know, the viral one that everybody's talking about; the one where mike just finished cleaning the toy store out, he comes home and he asks his big brothers[who are already shooting hoops together] to play twister with him and then they all proceed to tell him every other excuse in the world as to why they can't.

so what does mike do, you ask? michael goes and plays the game anyway, yet, he's not alone. he plays a game of twister with his baby chimp, bubbles.

it was in that moment in the dark of the theatre that twenty-something years of feelings, i’ve never been able to accurately describe nor transparently express came rushing through my veins like i stuck a needle in them jawns.

it was michael[and bubbles] who showed me what my loneliness truly looked like.

★

for the first time in my life the indescribable loneliness that i’ve always felt had been personified in the most poetic way; loneliness isn’t always being in sole isolation, true loneliness is the feeling of being surrounded by so many people and yet connecting to no one.

that was the recurring theme throughout the movie, if you ask me. outside of mike finding solace in his music, he found it within his animals instead of fellow humans and comrads.

i wish i could blame him but i can't because i know how that feels. i can remember feeling like that when i was looking out windows and imagining myself escaping to other worlds, where people looked at me with understanding eyes and patient smiles. it felt like a distant dream that i could only be apart of when i closed my eyes at night.

this aching pain of disconnection has fell upon me since adolescence. i don’t know where it came from or maybe that’s just my excuse because i’m too afraid to really bare my soul in this article. i don’t know if i want to put exact examples because that’ll make things feel too real. on the flipside of that, i'm probably worried about the public perception of what people will think of me, when they realize i don’t have many people around me i think i can trust.

there's people i’m worried about offending who wouldn’t dare call me on a random tuesday to wonder if i ate or even woke up that day. keeping up with me on social media is the only way they know what i look like now.

they can’t tell you the last time they checked on my wellbeing yet i’m the one they come to for all the answers to their problems. this is my reality but that dream world i talked about earlier? there’s a constant reciprocal energy resting in the belly of a pete rock beat loop.

i can only count on one hand the figures in my life that try to reach inside my heart and hug it with their warm essence. where is everyone else? i don’t know, and i so desperately wish i could tell you.

i know what you’re thinking: why is she so worried about people that don’t do for her yet barely giving light to the people that do? well my only answer to that would be because the people that don’t do for me are the main ones that always want something from me; my time, my wisdom, and my undying loyalty. but i can’t even get a happy birthday unless i’ve posted it on a 24-hour story.

maybe this article has become less about loneliness and more about how i’ve failed to create boundaries with people and to put on notice that my meter to please everyone has run out. it should be about that time in my life where my exhaustion is turning into pure rage over what i’ve allowed others to take from me without receiving anything in return.

in the morning, nobody is hitting my line besides shein and shapermint. my disconnection to others has a gap that falls even wider as i conquer my day with a smile and some laughter from the pits of my stomach.

there's parts of me that has just gotten use to it, so i'm numb but i'm content to a certain degree. meaning, i make due with what i have in life. i still try to have a good time, even if it's all in my head where scenarios place me at the center of everyone’s attention and love. in these fantasies, i dance just like michael but in reality, i face his same blues.

only difference between me and michael is that i don’t have a monkey to play twister with me, i just have the four walls of my room.
