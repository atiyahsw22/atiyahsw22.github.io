2dafuture: 6 next decade predictions

i overwhelmingly observe everything, so you have no choice but to read this because i'm forcing you.


★


oftentimes, i sit and think about how we are all the cause and effect of one another without realizing it. most of us as people think that we’re suffering and/or surviving through exclusive circumstances or we make irrational decisions, believing that it will only affect us on a personal level. we never think about how our actions will affect the next person, nor do we think about how trends affect legacies to come.

we’re shaped on the daily by a world that consumes us all at once. whether we follow said world order or not, we are forced to partake in the same experiences from different perspectives. it is the foundation of this earth where none of us will truly have had an individual experience, even if we never physically cross paths.

subconsciously, we all follow trends because society adjusts regardless.

[i can see, atiyah saleemah, 2026]

so here are six of my next decade predictions and where i believe we are heading in terms of culture, society, media, and entertainment.

[i am not a soothsayer, i just spend a lot of time on google and tiktok.]



[1. love or war]

the 2010s gave birth to the modern-day liberation movement. this movement switched societal gears from “earn this” to “everyone gets a gold star.” this way of life broke through to the majority and gave underrepresented communities an opportunity to be pushed into the spotlight. this "new world" truly began to make you feel like everyone’s voices could be heard and listened to.

in a non-fear-mongering way, i don't think we’ll see a time like that again because we are regressing. non-black america is yearning to revert back to a more “traditional” society (if you’re picking up what i’m putting down.) so as liberals will try harder to push envelopes, we will actually become even more conservative as a strong pushback to them. liberals will then offer the same pushback vice versa.

for example, there will be more religious depictions in movies, however, there will be people that will try to combat that and question the true meaning of it. the liberals will fight the conservatives on their opinions, and there will be a bridge of sorts to either try to work together or start a war. images will reflect one foot in this door and one foot in the other to represent conflict and spiritual warfare. people will be confused about what they’re actually watching and consuming because things won’t lean into either side too explicitly, but it will possibly force some people to pick sides, as neutrality will begin to become overwhelming.

think of the duality of madonna’s "like a prayer", the trailer of is god is, or the indecisive spirit of sammie in sinners.

edit: [mother, mother, mother is also now one to add to that list.]

[2. goin' back 2 da souf]

the roots of black america start in the south, so naturally, that’s where all roads will lead back to. there will be depictions of a pre-modern time before true freedom, yet with modern twists. they will try to make us feel better about where we’ve ended up as black people while showing just how far we still have to go.

this will prompt western imagery to be even bigger as it represents yesteryear. this shift i believe, started back when black people from the north, myself included, reverse-migrated to the south to start a new life circa mid-2010s. the domino effect then continued in movies like the harder they fall, nope, and eventually the rocket launcher that was sinners.

this imagery will even gain so much traction that it’ll be a catalyst for less extensive cosmetic work by actors. there will be some people who will say they can’t get work because they have things like too-obvious veneers or grand-scale body augmentations. influencers and social media personalities looking to transition into film and television will express the same sentiments; this will alter their usual vanity-driven personalities to focus more on the emoting and natural sensibility that their new profession will require.

music-wise, beyoncé's cowboy carter has already done some heavy lifting by introducing young ears to a country sound, and the rise of "boots on the ground," by 803fresh, shines a heavy light on southern line-dance culture. also, musicians like chris brown and fetty wap have adopted this style concept while tying in the current music sonics for their most recent music videos.

[3. let's get physical!]

what nas is doing with his record label mass appeal has been quite impactful and should be studied. the narrative around “legacy acts” has changed due to the innovation and evolution that he and mass appeal have been able to showcase, so i am not surprised by my next prediction and how he ties into this.

i saw on an instagram ad that mass appeal was releasing a cd pack, along with cassette tapes and other forms of physical media. this falls right in line with where we are heading in terms of occupying our time and keeping ourselves busy outside of scrolling all day. now, i’m not saying nas is the first artist to do this; i am just saying that he is the first artist i’ve personally seen implement selling old media within a new music marketing plan.

it’s smart, and it shows that he and mass appeal are keeping up with the musical joneses[no pun intended], the era of media regression, and the healthy rebirth of physical media, outside of the usual vinyl drops that an artist would release.

we’ve reached a certain peak of where social media can take us, so right now, as it stands, it doesn’t seem like there’s anywhere left for things to go besides back down. as stated in my introduction, we operate together more than we think, so collectively we all, at different times, have expressed exhaustion with social media and the advanced technology that keeps us connected consistently. i know that we would all love to disconnect from one another every once in a while.

every other day, i see a news headline shining light on this growing craze, which makes me think that hypothetically we will either face a blackout or a temporary shutdown of instant media. they’ve also made it a point to highlight the stories of pre-teens and teens gravitating towards physical media. so, that direction they're taking is not lost on me because everything hip and new reaches them first, then spreads throughout the growing ages.

[4. here we go again! (goin thru the same thangz)]

commercials and other forms of advertisements will bank on the feeling of nostalgia more than they've ever had before. aesthetic moodboards and edits on tiktok can be thanked for that as they praise past decades, which then contributes to viral sounds and recharting for some[miguel's "sure thing".] this emotion will be used for financial gain in some ways, which isn't a completely new concept in terms of behavioral science. i saw it when i was watching the oscars this year; almost every commercial referenced something of the past–especially the burger king one.

there will be retro references you can’t ignore and re-releases of products that either failed back in the day and now they’re trying their hand at it again, or re-releases of beloved items that were the most popular in said time. old toys, especially, will be the hottest thing on the market for not only kids, but also adults that are looking to heal their inner child and reconnect with their former self.

these re-releases won’t just stop at products; we are going to see companies attempt to recreate nostalgic spaces as well.

blockbuster will try its hand at pop-up shops or limited-time brick-and-mortars to gauge the soft launch of its return. pizza hut will become a big thing for high school kids to hang out after school or on the weekends, like they do at the malls. millennials will use pizza hut as a safe space to go with their babies to break up time on the ipad. the resurgence of the beloved pizza chain will drive communities of people back together that were separated by growing up on social media.

[5. the encore]

as a young child, i would sit in front of the television and watch 106 & park everyday like clockwork. it was around this time where tink, mack wilds, and elle varner were showcasing talent and standing on the frontlines of the “'90s is back” wave.

i’m telling you the truth when i say that, for a good part of the 2010s, that’s exactly what it felt like. i have a strong feeling that by mid-decade, this feeling will, in fact, return. the inevitable is that what goes around comes around, but what’s important to recognize is that by dipping into the 2010s, you would also have to revisit the '80s and '90s flavor that that era borrowed from and made their own.

illmatic had just reached 20 years and celebrated with an incredible documentary, time is illmatic, then you had “trillmatic” by a$ap nast ft. method man and "wu-tang forever" by drake, and i swear every time i turned on wdas with patty jackson or wrnb with lady b, the affordable old school concert was the place to be.

everyone from big daddy kane, doug e. fresh, and more were selling out shows, and flowers were being given out on the regular. tlc’s movie was popping, and the new edition story was in the making[i was super excited to see my guys’ story play out on the small screen.] you also couldn’t scroll down instagram without seeing radio raheem rings or bamboo earrings that channeled the quintessential around-the-way girl.

music will feel good again, that's all i'm trying to say. now i'm not one of those "there's no good music anymore" people; however, i do recognize that you have to dig in the crate a lot more to hear it versus it being mainstream. but i'm telling you, we will get back there, that's my whole point.

the turning point of this shift, i feel, happened when the lox won the verzuz against dipset. seeing their incredible showmanship had fans appreciating true artistry, and we were yearning for more developed stage presence, real personalities, and not made-up facades. the people just want artists to let go of viral gimmicks and internet fame-seeking mentalities because raw, authentic talent trumps it all. sheek, styles, and kiss proved it to us that night; it was sort of like an awakening.

i do believe that’s why kendrick’s gnx era got so much love, but that’s a whole other article.

[6. who's ur favorite member?]

this last and final prediction rests in the hearts of every fangirl and boy across the americas. with the rise and outstanding fame of k-pop groups like bts, newjeans, and katseye (i know they’re american-based, but they follow the k-pop formula and they’re signed to a korean label), american music corporations will want their slice of that pie eventually. it’s the inevitability of the capitalistic machine we all live in. [that was a tongue twister, wasn't it?]

girl and boy groups will experience a revival after we’ve been in a drought for so many years. the solo artist has been pushed on us because it’s more affordable in the long run for labels, whereas groups have disbanded for numerous reasons; most of the time, due to the financial strain. labels having to split costs and budgets amongst three or more members is not as easy as it sounds and it causes unmanageable friction.

so while it is expensive to put a group together versus developing one artist, music corporations will construct a new group deal that’s even cheaper than it was before [because let’s be real, they never paid groups good money in the first place; ask en vogue.]

they’ll get more bang for the buck this time, though, because they’ll market different personalities to different demographics and cash in on that like a bunch of solo artists in one group. it'll be a wu-tang kind of thing, but for r&b and pop acts. it's a very different concept compared to the spice girls, where everything circled back to the group and branding was centralized in the grand scheme.

believe you me, artist development isn’t as dead as you may assume. it just doesn’t happen behind the scenes anymore; it happens in the forms of leon thomas, sabrina carpenter, and victoria monét; these are the underdogs that everyone counted out until that one smash hit shot them to the moon. they worked hard for years, finding their sounds, writing for others, and developing in real time who they are today. this will be the same for upcoming groups.

there are packs of young girls and boys in a basement or garage practicing right now, preparing to take the world by storm.
